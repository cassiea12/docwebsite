The John Muir Trail

California

Stretching 211 miles from Yosemite Valley to the summit of the contiguous United States’ highest peak, Mount Whitney, it rambles through some of the most breathtaking mountain scenery that the heartand mind could conceive. Most of your days are spent above 10,000 feet, where you’ll experience the High Sierras in all of it’s grandeur and beauty. Construction of the trail began in 1915 after the passing of its namesake Sierra Club founder and conservationist ninja John Muir (whose writings and efforts are responsible for the protection of not only most of the lands that the trail goes through, but all protected wild places around the world.) The trail was completed in 1938.
